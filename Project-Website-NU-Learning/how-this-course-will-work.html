How This Course Will Work

Foundations Course

Introduction

NU-Learning is an open-source initiative aimed at offering a complete and free curriculum that helps students progress from zero experience to becoming full-stack developers.

In this section, we'll explore how the web functions and introduce you to the fundamentals of web development and computer programming.

Each lesson and unit covers essential foundational knowledge. Whether or not you plan on becoming a web developer, this material will provide valuable insights into how websites and web applications are created and delivered.

We'll begin by understanding the internet and familiarizing ourselves with your local machine. From there, we’ll set up a development environment, and introduce Git and GitHub, key tools for version control. Afterward, we’ll dive into front-end technologies such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, before taking a brief look at the back end and its essential concepts.

By the end of this section, you will not only grasp how the web works but also be able to identify and understand the various technologies used in web development. You will be able to create a simple webpage, style it, and implement interactive elements—all while gaining confidence in using the command line.

This section offers a broad overview to provide context. Before diving into more advanced topics, it's crucial to understand why certain technologies exist and how they play a role in web development.

Lesson overview

What You Will Learn:

  • Understand how NU-Learning’s curriculum works:
    Our curriculum is designed to take you step-by-step through the fundamentals of web development, from front-end basics to back-end concepts. Each lesson builds on the previous one to ensure that you develop a deep understanding of each topic.
  • Understand how the Foundations course works:
    The Foundations course introduces you to key concepts such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and version control with Git. You’ll set up your development environment and start working on simple projects to practice your skills as you progress through the lessons.

How it works

NU-Learning is an online platform designed to help students learn web development through structured courses. This curriculum collects the best resources from across the web to teach specific topics. In each lesson, we’ll introduce the topic and provide helpful context, guiding you to external resources created by industry experts.

Throughout your learning journey, you'll encounter questions to check your understanding and exercises to reinforce key concepts. Projects are embedded within the curriculum to allow you to apply your knowledge and grow your skills through hands-on experience.

Learning with NU-Learning isn’t like a traditional classroom. It’s a continuous process, where learning comes from doing. Think of yourself as a snowball rolling down a hill—gathering knowledge and experience along the way. Don’t worry if you forget things or feel unsure at times; this is all part of the learning process. The projects are not meant to test your memorization but to help you recall information as you work on solving problems, and you can always rely on the community for support.

A note about tools

We often get questions about why NU-Learning doesn’t cover specific tools or programming languages in detail.

The reason is simple: once you complete this curriculum, you won’t need a step-by-step tutorial for every tool or language. NU-Learning’s main goal is not to teach isolated skills but to help change the way you approach learning and problem-solving.

One of the biggest challenges you’ll face during this course is developing the right mindset. Even more challenging is learning the core ideas behind programming. It’s not just about writing code; it’s about solving problems, asking the right questions, and doing effective research. When you master this approach, expanding your skill set will feel natural. NU-Learning is here to help you build a strong foundation of these critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

After completing the course, you’ll be able to jump straight into the documentation of any tool or language. Learning how to navigate technical documentation is an essential skill, but it can feel overwhelming at first. Many resources assume you already have a programming background, which is why NU-Learning will guide you through understanding complex material on your own.

The skills you gain from NU-Learning will serve as the building blocks for your future growth. If you feel the need for another course after completing NU-Learning, it might mean you haven’t fully absorbed the key concepts here. The aim of this course is to give you confidence and a framework for continued learning.

Once you understand the core principles, finding how to apply them to new languages or tools becomes much easier. In most cases, all you’ll need is a Google search to fill in the details.

A note about human language

NU-Learning attracts students from around the world who are eager to become web developers. It’s important to note that this curriculum is primarily written in English, and maintained by English speakers. While we are not able to offer translations, it’s crucial to understand that the world of programming is deeply rooted in the English language. From programming syntax to documentation, the majority of resources you’ll encounter are written in English, and most of the global developer community also communicates in English.

If English is not your first language, don’t let this discourage you. Instead, view it as a preparation for the path ahead. To support your learning, we recommend taking extra time to review terms and concepts that may not be clear at first. Additionally, it’s a good idea to look for supplementary resources in your native language to deepen your understanding.

You might also want to keep a translation tool or dictionary handy to refer to as you work through the lessons. While we don’t provide specific recommendations, finding a reliable translation resource can be an excellent starting point. Searching for useful tools online is a key skill that will serve you well as you continue learning and developing your programming abilities.

What comes next

Once you’ve completed NU-Learning, you’ll have a solid understanding of the building blocks of web development and be eager to explore more advanced topics. While the course covers a broad range of important subjects, it’s just an introduction to the endless possibilities that await you in the world of web programming.

The final lesson of NU-Learning will allow you to choose between two paths: Full Stack JavaScript and Full Stack Rails. Both options are designed to build on the foundation you’ve established in this course, refining your skills into a highly capable toolkit for real-world development.

NU-Learning is maintained by experts who have carefully curated the best available resources and structured them into a clear, step-by-step guide. If suitable external resources don’t exist, we create our own to ensure that all the content is relevant and high-quality. Every lesson in the curriculum has been chosen deliberately to ensure your success as a programmer.

Each lesson builds upon the one before it, so skipping any part may leave gaps in your knowledge that can hinder your ability to solve future problems. That’s why it’s important to work through the material in order to fully grasp each concept.

We also provide additional resources for those who want to dive deeper into specific topics. These are optional unless otherwise stated but can help you develop a more thorough understanding of the subject matter.
