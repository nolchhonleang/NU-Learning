At NU-Learning, we offer two primary tracks after completing the Foundations Course:

•Full Stack JavaScript: This track focuses on using JavaScript for both front-end and back-end development. You’ll dive deeper into HTML, CSS, and JavaScript on the front end, and then learn how to build server-side applications using Node.js, Express, and PostgreSQL for the back end. This track is great for those who want to master one language and use it across the entire stack.

•Full Stack Ruby on Rails: In this track, you’ll start by learning Ruby, a programming language known for its simplicity and readability. You’ll then move into Ruby on Rails, a powerful framework for building web applications quickly and efficiently. This track also covers front-end technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, but it emphasizes Ruby for back-end development.

Both tracks provide a comprehensive understanding of full-stack development, so the choice depends on which technology excites you more. JavaScript is widely used across the industry, while Ruby on Rails is valued for its rapid development capabilities.

ILearning to code can be challenging, but staying motivated is key to success. Here are a few tips to keep you on track:

Set Short-Term Goals: Break down your learning into smaller, achievable milestones. Completing a project or mastering a specific skill will give you a sense of progress.

Join a Study Group: Learning with others can make the journey more enjoyable and keep you accountable. NU-Learning’s community is filled with learners at all levels, so you’ll always find someone to study or collaborate with.

Celebrate Small Wins: Every time you solve a problem or complete a project, take a moment to celebrate your achievement. This positive reinforcement will help maintain your momentum.

Seek Support: If you ever feel stuck, reach out to the community or a mentor. Don’t be afraid to ask questions—everyone was a beginner at some point, and the NU-Learning community is here to help.
